## AI Security Engineer, MLSecOps, and AI Governance: Cybersecurity Job Market Analysis (2024)

**Current Trends and Developments**

The cybersecurity workforce in 2024 is experiencing significant shifts due to the rapid integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technologies. The demand for AI and ML talent is surging, with the AI market projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 37.3% from 2023 to 2030[4]. This expansion is fueling the creation of new roles at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity, such as AI Security Engineer, MLSecOps (Machine Learning Security Operations), and AI Governance specialists.

**Key Trends:** - **Emergence of New Roles:** AI is driving the need for specialized positions like AI Security Engineer and MLSecOps, focusing on securing AI/ML pipelines and ensuring the integrity of AI systems[2][4]. - **Governance and Compliance:** The complexity of AI-related regulations is increasing demand for cybersecurity/privacy attorneys and professionals focused on AI governance[5]. - **Market Recovery and Stabilization:** After notable declines in certain cybersecurity roles from 2022 to 2024, there are early signs of stabilization and even modest rebounds in areas like Product Security Engineering and DevSecOps[5].

## Conclusion

While some traditional cybersecurity positions have experienced declines, the market is showing signs of stabilization and recovery, particularly in areas related to AI and regulatory compliance.
