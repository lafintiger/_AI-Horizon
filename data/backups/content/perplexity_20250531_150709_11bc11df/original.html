Free Online Cybersecurity Workforce Certification Training

PNW is proud to introduce highly qualified professionals into the cybersecurity workforce. Register for this free online certification training to enhance your career opportunities! This program is only open to USA citizens and USA permanent residents.

The CWCT application is currently closed. If the program re-opens, this page will be updated in July 2025

Registration

The CWCT program is open for new applicants for the 2024 training opportunities.

Visit Steps To Apply page has details on submitting an application.

Ask a Question

Training Paths

From system administration to digital forensics, discover which training path is right for you. The 10-week non-credit college level courses are offered online with virtual live lectures at a scheduled time.

Explore Programs

Programs

Cybersecurity-Artificial Intelligence (CS_AI)

This track offers six courses that cover the fundamentals of computer systems, networks and cybersecurity, as well as topics in fundamentals of artificial intelligence, IoT and hardware security.

Cybersecurity-System Administration (CS_SA)

This track offers six courses that cover the fundamentals of computer systems, networks and cybersecurity, as well as advanced topics in cyber infrastructure development and security protection.

Cybersecurity-Digital Forensics (CS_DF)

This track offers six courses that cover the fundamentals of computer systems, networks and cybersecurity, as well as advanced topics in cyber forensics investigation.

Cybersecurity Workforce Development

This projects is supported by a National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity Grant, housed at the National Security Agency. #H98230-20-1-0351

Since 2014, Purdue University Northwest has been a National Center of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense Education (CAE-CDE 4Y) designated jointly by the NSA and the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS).

Partners

Purdue University Northwest in collaboration with Ivy Tech Community College, University of North Carolina at Charlotte (UNCC), and University of Tennessee at Chattanooga (UTC), will establish a pilot Artificial Intelligence (AI)-Cybersecurity certification based national training program for transitioning military, first responders, and other adult trainees.
